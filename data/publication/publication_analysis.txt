TITLE (working):
  Systematic cross-species selectivity analysis reveals SmDHODH
  as the most tractable target for schistosomiasis drug repurposing

ABSTRACT (working):
  Drug repurposing offers a fast path to new schistosomiasis
  treatments, but candidate prioritization requires assessment of
  selectivity against human orthologues — a step frequently omitted
  in computational repurposing studies. We built an integrated pipeline
  combining target-based bioactivity (ChEMBL), whole-organism evidence,
  structural similarity, ADMET properties, and cross-species selectivity
  to systematically evaluate the schistosomiasis repurposing landscape.
  Of 73 SmHDAC8 compounds with human orthologue
  data, 90.4% showed poor or counter-selectivity,
  including all clinically advanced HDAC inhibitors (vorinostat,
  panobinostat, quisinostat).
  In contrast, SmDHODH yielded 3 compounds with
  >10-fold parasite selectivity, with the best achieving
  30.83x (IC50 = 23 nM, QED = 0.89).
  Atovaquone, an approved antimalarial available in sub-Saharan Africa,
  showed 6-fold SmDHODH selectivity, supporting its prioritization
  for anti-schistosomal evaluation.
